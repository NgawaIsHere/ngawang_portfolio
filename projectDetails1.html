Project Overview

The Product

FurniQuest is a modern online furniture store that offers customizable furniture suited to Bhutanese home and office environments. Customers can browse collections, customize materials or sizes, and place orders from the comfort of their homes. The app aims to bridge the gap between traditional furniture shopping and the convenience of e-commerce.

Project information

  • Category: Responsive Web Design
  • Project Duration: Feb - June 2024
View on Figma

The Problem Statement

Many Bhutanese customers find it challenging to purchase customized furniture due to limited online availability, lack of visualization tools, and delays in communication with carpenters or vendors. This results in unsatisfactory purchases or the need for physical visits.

The Goal

To design an intuitive e-commerce platform that enables users to explore, customize, and order furniture easily. This includes allowing custom orders, delivery tracking, and online payment to enhance user convenience and satisfaction.

Role

UX/UI Designer. [UI (user interface), UX (user experience)]

1. Empathize (Understanding the Users)

User research: Summary

Through interviews and empathy mapping, we identified key user pain points: difficulty finding suitable furniture on cluttered sites, limited customization options, uncertainty around delivery timelines, poor communication with sellers, and the inconvenience of needing in-person visits for design confirmation—especially for remote users.

User Research: Pain Point

01

Product Discovery

Users struggle to find the right furniture because current websites are cluttered or outdated.

02

Customization

Customers want to customize furniture, but current platforms offer limited options.

03

Delivery Uncertainty

Lack of clear delivery timelines or tracking causes customer anxiety and confusion.

04

Communication Gap

Users want clearer, faster communication with the seller, especially for custom orders.

05

In-person Dependency

Many purchases require physical visits to confirm design or materials, which is inconvenient for users in remote areas.

2. Define

(HMW Questions and Point of View Statements)

Problem Statements (How Might We...)

  • How might we allow customers to customize furniture orders based on their space and preferences?
  • How might we improve delivery transparency for large and fragile items like furniture?
  • How might we make product discovery faster and more relevant?
  • How might we simplify custom furniture ordering without in-person visits?
  • How might we enhance trust and communication between seller and buyer during customization?
  • How might we reduce user drop-off during checkout?
  • How might we help customers visualize furniture in their homes before purchasing?
  • How might we offer better post-purchase support for large item installation or returns?

Paper wireframe

I created paper wireframes for the homepage, upload, login, and signup pages to experiment with different ways of organizing the information. I took notes on each screen about what worked well and used those insights to develop the digital low-fidelity wireframes.



Digital wireframe

Next, I transitioned to Figma to design digital wireframes for all the screens of FurniQuest. By applying a basic design system, I crafted low-fidelity wireframes that were clean, intuitive, and user-friendly. The homepage serves as a central hub, allowing users to browse featured furniture collections and access their profile or cart. The bottom navigation bar includes options to explore categories, customize products, track orders, and seamlessly navigate between the app’s main sections.

Accessibility Considerations

  1. Used clear icons and consistent layouts to support easy navigation for all users.
  2. Included visual cues and tooltips to assist users unfamiliar with customization terms and options.
  3. Designed the app to accommodate users with disabilities by enabling online ordering without needing to visit physical stores.
  4. Ensured text hierarchy and contrast to improve readability and comprehension across different user groups.
  5. Planned intro walkthrough to help first-time users quickly understand app features and reduce confusion.
